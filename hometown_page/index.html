Brief History

Cavite came from the word “Kawit” which means hook signifying our land is a shape of peninsula which used to be a sea port of Spaniards. People who live in Cavite are called “Caviteños“ who are also known for their bravery. They are one of the people who participated in Death March during Japanese colonization in World War 2. Because of that, one of the eight rays of the sun in the Philippine flag symbolizes the place.




Brave Heroes of Cavite

Three Martyr Priests

GomBurZa (Mariano Gomez, Jose Burgos & Jacinto Zamora) are the three-martyr priest who sacrificed their lives in 1872 during Spanish colonization. They were executed using a garrote at Bagumbayan.Most people in Cavite speaks Tagalog which is our national dialect. However, there are some who speaks Chavacano which is a broken Spanish.


First President of the Philippines

Another historical fact, Cavite is where General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first President of the Philippines, came from. He led the surprise attack at the Spanish headquarter. Aguinaldo was exiled but he came back bringing the victory and independence.
